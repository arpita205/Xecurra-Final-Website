Trade Glossary

Confused by the jargon? Here are the most common trade terms explained simply.

Bill of Lading (BOL)
A document issued by a carrier to acknowledge receipt of cargo for shipment. It serves as a receipt, contract of carriage, and document of title.
Letter of Credit (LC)
A letter from a bank guaranteeing that a buyer's payment to a seller will be received on time and for the correct amount.
FCL / LCL
Full Container Load (you rent the entire container) vs. Less than Container Load (you share container space with other shippers).
RoDTEP
Remission of Duties and Taxes on Exported Products — an Indian government scheme that refunds hidden central/state taxes to exporters.
Customs House Agent (CHA)
A licensed agent who acts on behalf of the importer/exporter to handle customs clearance procedures.
Proforma Invoice
A preliminary bill of sale sent to buyers in advance of a shipment or delivery of goods, detailing the cost.
